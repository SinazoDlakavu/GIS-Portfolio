Terrain and Geological Risk for Built-Up Areas – South of Mthatha

Objective

To identify built-up areas situated on steep slopes (>15°) in relation to underlying geological formations in the Mthatha region. This analysis supports land-use planning by highlighting areas where slope and geology may present potential development risks.

Method Summary

  • Clipped the 1:1 million South African geology layer to the Mthatha municipal extent
  • Loaded a DEM and derived slope in degrees using the terrain analysis tool
  • Reclassified slope raster to identify steep slopes greater than 15°
  • Extracted built-up areas from the 2022 SA Land Cover dataset and reclassified to a binary raster
  • Overlayed built-up and steep slope rasters to highlight risk zones
  • Styled final output over the geology layer for visual interpretation of geological context

Focus Area

King Sabata Dalindyebo District Municipality, Eastern Cape

Tools Used

  • QGIS 3.34.4 (Münster)
  • Raster Calculator
  • Slope (Terrain Analysis) Tool
  • Clip by Mask Layer
  • Reclassify by Table (Raster)
  • SA National Land Cover 2022 (NLC)
  • 1:1 Million Geological Map of South Africa (CGS)
  • South African Municipal Boundaries (Municipal Demarcation Board)
  • Google Earth (for context and place names)
